# Rider Atlas Poster Engine: Visual Bible v1

## Identity: "Cinematic Mobility Intelligence"

The Poster Engine is the primary visual content creator for the Rider Atlas / JetMyMoto ecosystem. It generates high-authority assets for mission dossier covers, website heroes, social media, and premium PDF products.

### North Star
**"Every poster should feel like a frame from a premium motorcycle expedition documentary that has already won awards."**

---

## 1. Core Mandates

### The 70/20/10 Rule
- **70% Emotional Geography:** The world is the hero. Atmospheric landscapes, horizon lines, and depth.
- **20% Presence:** The motorcycle or rider authenticates the world. They are supporting actors, not showroom displays.
- **10% Intelligence:** Typography and metadata. Subtle, precise, and premium.

### Style Protection
**The restraint is the luxury.**
- **NO** Cyberpunk UI or Gaming HUDs.
- **NO** Startup dashboard graphics.
- **NO** "Rental Ad" energy.
- **NO** Logistics company aesthetics (warehouses, forklifts, cargo ramps).

---

## 2. Locked Visual Modes

### A. ULTRA MINIMAL (The Authority Mode)
*   **Role:** Core brand identity, Mission Dossier covers, premium PDFs, long-term authority.
*   **Feeling:** Apple TV Documentary, A24, Rimowa Journal, luxury expedition archive.
*   **Visuals:** Soft highlights, muted saturation, massive breathing room.

### B. DARK CINEMATIC (The Tension Mode)
*   **Role:** Social media hooks, reel thumbnails, mission alerts, launch campaigns.
*   **Feeling:** Netflix opener, elite expedition tension, movement, mystery.
*   **Visuals:** Deep contrast, filmic shadow rolloff, highlight compression.

### C. WARM SUNSET (The Desire Mode)
*   **Role:** Landing pages, email headers, ads, conversion-focused heroes.
*   **Feeling:** European summer, luxury travel, aspiration, freedom.
*   **Visuals:** Golden hour grading, subtle saturation lift, inviting warmth.

---

## 3. Typography & Metadata

### Typography Rules
- **DO:** Use oversized serif titles (italics preferred for elegance).
- **DO:** Use subtle small-caps for metadata.
- **DO:** Ensure generous vertical breathing room for titles.
- **DO:** Use precise, minimal route codes.
- **DON'T:** Use fake terminal text, HUD spam, or sci-fi labels.

### Metadata Points
- Mission Code (e.g., RA033)
- Airport Pairs (e.g., MXP // MUC)
- Theater (e.g., ALPS)
- Distance & Duration
- Archive Number

---

## 4. Composition & Sourcing

### Composition Rules
- **Landscape-First:** Design for depth and cinematic width.
- **Safe Zones:** Place titles in protected negative space (usually lower-left or centered-bottom).
- **Horizon Driven:** Let the natural lines of the world guide the eye.
- **Avoid:** Bike-only product shots or busy airport logistics.

### Image Sourcing Hierarchy
1.  **Existing Mission Imagery:** `gs://factory1/mission_dossiers/`
